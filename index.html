Interpreting Positive COVID-19 Antibody Tests

A number of large-scale studies are underway to understand the prevalence of COVID-19 antibodies in various populations. As with any medical test, antibody testing is not infallible. Some people without the antibody will test positive (a false positive) and some with the antibody will test negative (a false negative). This tool can be used to interpret the meaning of an individual positive test result in the context of a population study. That interpretation depends not only on the accuracy of the test, but also on the prevalence of the antibody in the population being tested. Even with high accuracy testing, anytime the false positive rate exceeds the prevalence, most positive test results will be incorrect. This is known as the false positive paradox.
